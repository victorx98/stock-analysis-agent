Market Correction Risk And Undervalued Sectors

A market-wide research brief on correction risk, the AI bubble question, undervalued sectors, individual stock research candidates, and safer places to hide if the market sells off.

Run date: 2026-05-17 Horizon: 6-24 months Classification: research support only

Plain-English Bottom Line

Section detail ->

Normal correction risk is high; a 2008-style crash is not the base case. My rough research estimate is a 45-60% probability of a 10%+ S&P 500 correction in the next 12 months, a 20-30% probability of a 20%+ bear market, and a 10-15% probability of a deeper crash. The deeper-crash path most likely needs multiple shocks at once: AI capex confidence breaks, long-term rates rise or stay high, credit spreads widen, and forced selling hits crowded risk assets.

The best hunting ground is not the hottest AI hardware trade. It is quality cash-flow businesses where fear is already in the price: healthcare, select REITs, select financials, communication platforms, and software names where AI disruption fears have outrun evidence.

Why The Market Can Correct

Section detail ->
Risk Evidence Interpretation
Crowded AI valuation and capex expectations The Federal Reserve's May 2026 Financial Stability Report flags risk-asset correction risk tied to AI valuation concerns and debt-funded AI capex. AI revenue is real, but the public-market pricing of chips, power, data centers, and growth software may assume too much perfect execution.
Fiscal debt and rate pressure CBO/CRFB data show debt held by the public projected near 120% of GDP by 2036, with net interest projected above $2T by 2036. Debt is usually a slow valuation headwind, not an automatic crash trigger. It becomes acute if bond buyers demand higher yields during an equity selloff.
Private credit and refinancing stress The Fed report continues to discuss leverage, credit quality, and vulnerabilities outside traditional banks. This is a hidden-transmission risk. Public equities may not price it until defaults or fund liquidity issues become visible.
Market concentration Price action through 2026-05-15 showed QQQ and XLK strongly outperforming most equal-weight and defensive sectors. A few winning themes can keep indexes high while cheaper sectors lag. If the leaders break, index downside can be sharper than broad averages suggest.

Berkshire And The Cash Signal

Section detail ->

Berkshire's caution is real. Its Q1 2026 filing says insurance and other businesses held $373.5B of cash, cash equivalents, and U.S. Treasury bills net of unsettled purchases at March 31, 2026. The same filing shows $305.7B in equity and fixed-maturity securities, excluding equity-method investments.

Interpretation: Berkshire is not saying every stock is uninvestable. It is saying optionality is valuable when expected returns are not compelling enough. That supports a barbell: keep liquid cash/T-bills for future forced selling, while researching quality names that already price in fear.

AI Bubble Check

Section detail ->

The AI story is not fake. OpenAI reported about $2B/month in revenue and more than 900M weekly ChatGPT users in its March 2026 update. Anthropic said run-rate revenue surpassed $30B in April 2026. Those are real business signals.

The bubble risk is not "AI has no demand." The risk is that investors extrapolate real demand into unrealistic margins, unlimited data-center returns, excessive chip orders, and high software multiples for companies whose AI monetization is still unproven.

AI Bucket Research Judgment What Would Change It
Frontier model demand Real and large, based on reported OpenAI and Anthropic run-rate scale. Revenue growth decelerates while compute costs and capex commitments keep rising.
Semiconductor/data-center beneficiaries Powerful but crowded. Valuation risk is high when expectations require persistent scarcity and huge capex returns. Order cancellations, lower utilization, or falling GPU/data-center pricing.
Software incumbents Stock-by-stock. Some are AI winners, some are disrupted, many are still unproven. Retention, backlog, usage, and AI add-on annual contract value show durable monetization.

Safer Bets If A Big Correction Happens

Section detail ->
  • Short-duration Treasuries, T-bills, and high-quality money-market funds: best dry powder. This is the closest analog to Berkshire's current liquidity posture.
  • Berkshire Hathaway: not cheap by default, but a practical all-weather compounder with liquidity, insurance float, and opportunistic capital allocation.
  • Quality healthcare: usually less cyclical than tech or discretionary, but stock selection matters because policy and patent risks are real.
  • Selected profitable platforms: high free cash flow and net cash can survive selloffs better than speculative growth.
  • Gold/TIPS: useful fiscal/inflation hedges, but not automatically cheap after strong price moves.

Avoid calling long-duration bonds a universal safe bet. They help if recession drives rates lower. They can fail if the correction is caused by inflation, debt concerns, or a long-rate shock.
