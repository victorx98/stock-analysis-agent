This page records the filing evidence used to interpret Berkshire's cash posture as optionality, not as proof that every stock is too expensive.

Research Read

Berkshire's cash position supports a cautious market read because one of the most patient capital allocators is accepting T-bill returns over aggressive equity deployment. The stronger inference is not "sell everything"; it is "cash has strategic value when future expected returns may improve after volatility."

What Would Change The Read

Material Berkshire buybacks, major public-equity purchases, or a sharp drawdown that Berkshire uses to deploy cash would suggest the opportunity set has improved. A rise in cash alone would not prove a crash is coming.
