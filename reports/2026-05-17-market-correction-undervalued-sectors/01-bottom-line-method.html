Plain-English Bottom Line: Working Method

This page records how the headline correction-risk range and sector preference were assembled from macro evidence, market-price evidence, and valuation/quality filters.

Conclusion Carried Into Main Report

Normal correction risk is elevated, but a deep crash is not the base case. The highest-value action is to keep liquidity while building a watchlist of cash-flow businesses where fear is already priced in.

Why Healthcare Ranked First

The sector ranking combined three checks: whether recent price action already reflected some fear, whether the business demand base is defensive, and whether balance-sheet or policy risks are too large to call the whole sector cheap. Healthcare had the best mix. REITs and banks were interesting but more rate/credit sensitive. Energy had good hedge value but was less clearly cheap after strong momentum.

Limits

This was a market-wide scan, not a complete stock-by-stock valuation run. Candidate stocks are research queue items, not final signals. A directional stock signal still requires primary filings, current valuation inputs, leadership review, buyback/insider review, and trend review.
